{% endblock%} {% block title %}How to Use the Calculator{% endblock %} {% block content %}At the top of the calculator there are three preloaded scenarios. Scenario one shows current trends, scenario two shows quadratic growth of renewable energy, and scenario three shows increased prevalence of winterized natural gas.
Enter the Start Year for the projected graphs, the minimum year is 2021.
Enter the End Year for the projected graphs, this must be at least one year later than the imputed Start Year.
Enter the initial percentage of electricity generated by each source. There are six different sources, be sure that all the percentages add up to 100%.
Enter the change in percentage of electricity generated by each source. In this section Natural Gas is used as the slack value, use this blank to ensure the percentages add up to 100%.
Enter the change in the change in percent of electricity generated by each source. This value is the squared value of the percentages entered in the section above, change in percentage of electricity generated by each source.
Enter the percent effectiveness of winterized natural gas pipes.
Press “Submit”
Choose between the six different graphs at the top of the page in the blue boxes. The graphs show: energy production, carbon emissions, energy generation distributions, reliability, construction costs, and consumer price.
To go back to the calculator and input different numbers, use the blue “Back to the Calculator!” button located in the bottom left corner of the page. {% endblock %}
